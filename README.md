## 📋 Project Overview

This is a comprehensive **Excel Dashboard** that analyzes **2,465 applications** for the 2026 Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters in Mathematics programmes (InterMaths, Math Modelling, and Real Math).

The dashboard provides clear insights into applicant nationality, program selections, scholarship decisions (Awarded, Awaiting, N/A), and selection patterns across countries.

**Key Highlights:**
- Total Candidates: **2,465**
- Awarded Full Scholarships: **20**
- Awaiting Decision: **110**
- Triple Yes (Selected in all 3 programmes): **130**
- Top Nationality: **Pakistan** (35 applicants considered for decision)
- Strongest representation from Nigeria (30) and Cameroon (29)

---

## 🎯 Business / Analytical Objectives

- Understand the competitiveness and geographical distribution of applicants
- Analyze program selection patterns (InterMaths, Math Modelling, Real Math)
- Track scholarship decision status and success rate
- Identify top-performing countries and applicant profiles
- Support data-driven insights for future application cycles

---

## 💡 Strategic Value & Implementation

**Why This Dashboard is Useful:**

This dashboard transforms raw application data into strategic intelligence. It enables the InterMaths consortium, partner universities, and future applicants to better understand the dynamics of one of Europe’s most competitive scholarship programmes.

**Key Benefits:**
- Provides instant visibility into global participation and success rates
- Highlights geographical strengths and under-represented regions
- Reveals the extreme competitiveness of the programme (only **0.81%** success rate)
- Helps identify patterns in programme selection and scholarship allocation
- Serves as a powerful communication tool for transparency and reporting

**How It Can Be Used for Re-strategizing:**

- **Recruitment Strategy**: Target under-represented high-potential regions with tailored outreach campaigns
- **Application Support**: Develop country-specific guidance based on performance patterns
- **Scholarship Allocation**: Use insights to review and optimize future selection criteria and quotas
- **Diversity Goals**: Monitor and improve geographical and gender balance in future intakes
- **Marketing & Visibility**: Share success stories from high-performing countries to attract stronger future applicants
- **Predictive Planning**: Establish benchmarks for expected application volume and quality in subsequent years

This type of data-driven approach can significantly improve the efficiency, fairness, and global reach of future EMJM application cycles.

## 📊 Key Insights

### Decision Status Overview

| Status              | Count   | Percentage |
|---------------------|---------|------------|
| Awarded             | 20      | 0.81%     |
| Awaiting            | 110     | 4.46%     |
| N/A                 | 189     | 7.67%     |
| Triple No           | 2,146   | 87.06%    |

### Other Findings
- **130 candidates** were selected in all three programmes (Triple Yes)
- European countries (Italy, Spain, Germany, Croatia) have fewer but highly competitive applications
- Only **0.81%** of total applicants received the full EMJM Scholarship

**Note**: This analysis is based on the official selection list released by Erasmus Mundus. No causal conclusions should be drawn regarding admission criteria.
